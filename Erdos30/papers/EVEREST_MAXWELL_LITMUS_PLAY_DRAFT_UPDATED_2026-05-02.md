# Everest, or Maxwell's Litmus

Draft date: 2026-04-30
Status: internal dramatic draft
Claim boundary: family/story artifact, not a mathematical proof artifact

## Author's Note

This play is not a transcript. It is a staged version of a real research
tension: a father trying to make a serious dynamical-systems program legible in
the age of AI, while his estranged son and a credentialed skeptic see only the
worst possible surface: a pre-alpha interface, exposed first-day vocabulary,
physics words near famous mathematics, and no patient conversation.

In the play, the Erdos work is not the original mountain. It begins as a side
ridge: a way to get something more objective than self-description, something
that exact finite packets and Lean certificates could make harder to dismiss.
That side ridge becomes its own nightmare, because to a skeptic it looks almost
designed to confirm every fear about AI-era overclaiming.

The mathematical claim ceiling is deliberately narrow. The work described here
does not prove Erdos #30. It shows exact finite structure around Sidon
maximizer faces, including a packet-backed handoff at `n = 57`, a compiled
finite Lean certificate over the Sidon `n = 20..30` field-response window, and
a compiled `n = 57/58` branch certificate.

The human claim is narrower still: misunderstanding is not proof of madness,
and conviction is not proof of correctness. The only honest court is evidence.

## Characters

KEN: A father, inventor, and outsider researcher trying to earn credibility for
AI-assisted work in dynamical systems and biomedical signal interpretation,
while meeting institutional skepticism from nearly every direction.

ANDRE: His estranged son. Not a mathematician: a former Princeton math student
who left mathematics for Latin. He knows enough to distrust grand claims, enough
to feel superior to bad packaging, and enough to be dangerous in both
directions.

THE FRIEND: An award-winning mathematician, never seen directly. His judgment
arrives through Andre after seeing a pre-alpha surface, not after a careful
conversation.

CODEX: An AI research instrument. Not an oracle. Not a muse. A disciplined
assistant with a terminal, a compiler, and no right to believe anything until
the packet exists.

FEYNMAN: A ghostly presence. Not Richard Feynman exactly, but the spirit of
explaining things by making them honest.

MAXWELL: A second ghost. Carries a notebook full of missing terms.

THE MOUNTAIN: Everest, represented by light, altitude, and thin air.

THE FACE: A six-point mathematical object at `n = 57`, played by six silent
figures who rearrange themselves under different fields.

## Setting

A room that is also a mountain camp.

Downstage: a table with a laptop, printed packets, a terminal window, and a
draft titled `Mendoza's Limit`.

Upstage: a translucent map of Everest. Altitude marks glow as the scenes
advance: 3,000 meters, 5,500 meters, 7,000 meters, 8,000 meters.

At stage right: a child's chair, empty.

At stage left: six figures in gray, the exact maximizer face.
